Charting Project by Samantha Perry

Russell Wilson and the Seattle Seahawks are off to their best start yet, but the team continues to fall short in several aspects. Wilson has established himself as an MVP candidate, but the 'Hawks still put more pointa on the board when they pound the ball with their star running back, Chris Carson. The team has also struggled to get going in the third quarter and turnovers have given their opponents unnecessary advantage.

These charts analyze the preformance of the Seahawks in their first five games of the season.

Effect of rushing yards on total points scored

The Seahawks have struggled to find their run game since the departure of Marshawn Lynch but Chris Carson has finally found his footing. It is fancinating to see that the total points scored follows the total rushing yards rather than passing. Even though Russell Wilson has put on an MVP preformance so far this year, it is clear they need to keep feeding Carson the ball.

Total offensive yards compared to penalty yards

The Seahawks have been struggling after the first half. After coming out of the locker room, Seattle has had both fewer yards of offense and more yards lost to penalties.

Drive outcome

Seattle has notoriously short drives and is currently ranked 32nd in time of possession. Most drives end with a punt, but several high-scoring games have improved the ratio. Unforetunately, the turnover rate for the 'Hawks is also very notable, with both RB Chris Carson and WR Jaron Brown dealing with some fumble issues.
